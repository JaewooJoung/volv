Chinese Tea Classification

Green Tea (绿茶 - Lǜchá)

The most common type of tea, made without fermentation. There are steaming and roasting methods. Produced throughout China, especially famous in Zhejiang and Sichuan.

  • 碧螺春 (Bìluóchūn)
    "Green Snail Spring" - Known for its tight spiral shape and fruity aroma from Jiangsu province.
  • 毛峰 (Máofēng)
    "Fur Peak" - Named for its fuzzy white tea buds, a famous variety from Huangshan.
  • 瓜片 (Guāpiàn)
    "Melon Seed" - Unique flat, seed-shaped leaves without buds or stems from Anhui.
  • 银针 (Yínzhēn)
    "Silver Needle" - Fine, needle-shaped white buds that produce a pale, delicate liquor.
  • 毛尖 (Máojiān)
    "Fur Tip" - Small, delicate tea with fuzzy buds, known for its sweet aftertaste.
  • 云雾 (Yúnwù)
    "Cloud and Mist" - Grown at high altitudes in misty mountains, known for its subtle flavor.

Red Tea (红茶 - Hóngchá)

Unlike green tea, it undergoes fermentation process, resulting in slightly reddish tea liquor.

  • 祁红 (Qíhóng) - Anhui
    "Keemun" - Famous black tea with wine-like and fruity flavors, known as the "Burgundy of teas".
  • 镇红 (Zhènhóng) - Yunnan
    "Dianhong" - Yunnan black tea with golden buds, known for its malty flavor and bright color.
  • 宣红 (Xuānhóng) - Hubei
    "Yichang black tea" - Hubei province black tea with a smooth, rich character.
  • 川红 (Chuānhóng) - Sichuan
    "Sichuan black tea" - Known for its sweet potato aroma and orange-yellow liquor.

Oolong(Black Dragon) Tea (乌龙茶 - Wūlóngchá)

Also known as blue tea. It undergoes partial fermentation where natural fermentation is stopped midway with artificial heat to dry tea leaves.

  • 铁观音 (Tiěguānyīn)
    "Iron Goddess of Mercy" - Most famous oolong with orchid aroma and complex aftertaste.
  • 白毛猴 (Báimáohóu)
    "White-haired Monkey" - Light oolong with twisted leaves resembling monkey paws.
  • 凤凰水仙 (Fènghuáng Shuǐxiān)
    "Phoenix Mountain Narcissus" - Famous for its honey and floral aroma from Guangdong.
  • 黄金桂 (Huángjīnguì)
    "Golden Cassia" - Early-harvest oolong with a distinctive peach-like aroma.

Flower Tea (花茶 - Huāchá)

Typically made by adding flower fragrance to green tea. Especially popular among Beijing people.

  • 茉莉花茶 (Mòlì Huāchá)
    "Jasmine Tea" - Green tea scented with jasmine flowers, China's most popular scented tea.
  • 玫瑰花茶 (Méiguī Huāchá)
    "Rose Tea" - Tea scented with rose petals, known for its romantic fragrance.
  • 白兰花茶 (Báilán Huāchá)
    "White Orchid Tea" - Tea infused with the delicate aroma of white orchid flowers.
  • 柚花茶 (Yòu Huāchá)
    "Pomelo Flower Tea" - Rare tea scented with pomelo blossoms, with citrus notes.
  • 金银花茶 (Jīnyín Huāchá)
    "Honeysuckle Tea" - Made with honeysuckle flowers, known for medicinal properties.

White Tea (白茶 - Báichá)

Dried using natural sunlight without artificial heat, with fermentation degree generally below 10%.

  • 白牡丹 (Báimǔdān)
    "White Peony" - Made from one bud and two leaves, with a sweet, mellow flavor.
  • 贡眉 (Gòngméi)
    "Tribute Eyebrow" - Made from larger leaves than other white teas, with stronger flavor.
  • 银针白毫 (Yínzhēn Báiháo)
    "Silver Needle White Hair" - Premium white tea made only from buds covered in white hairs.

Yellow Tea (黄茶 - Huángchá)

Tea with dark yellow color like barley tea, with fermentation degree between green tea and oolong tea.

  • 北港毛尖 (Běigǎng Máojiān)
    "Beigang Maojian" - Hunan yellow tea with a unique "menhuang" (smothering yellow) process.
  • 广东大叶青 (Guǎngdōng Dàyèqīng)
    "Guangdong Large Leaf Green" - Yellow tea from Guangdong with large leaves and mellow taste.
  • 海马宫茶 (Hǎimǎ Gōng Chá)
    "Haimagong Tea" - Rare yellow tea from Guizhou with a chestnut-like aroma.
  • 君山银针 (Jūnshān Yínzhēn)
    "Junshan Silver Needle" - Premium yellow tea where leaves stand upright when brewed.

Black Tea (黑茶 - Hēichá)

Tea fermented before tea leaves completely dry. Particularly famous is Yunnan's Pu'er tea.

  • 普洱茶 (Pǔ'ěr Chá)
    "Pu'er Tea" - Famous fermented tea from Yunnan that improves with age like fine wine.
  • 普洱紧压茶 (Pǔ'ěr Jǐnyā Chá)
    "Compressed Pu'er" - Pu'er tea pressed into cakes, bricks, or other shapes for aging.
